## Investment Thesis

- **Structural AI infrastructure play:** NVIDIA's Data Center segment ($115B, 88% of FY2025 revenue) is the dominant supplier of GPU compute for large-scale AI model training and inference. The H100/H200/Blackwell product cycle creates a multi-year demand runway with no credible near-term substitute.
- **Margin durability at scale:** FY2025 gross margin of 75.0% and EBIT margin of 62.4% — unprecedented for a hardware company at this revenue scale — reflect software-defined pricing power (CUDA ecosystem lock-in) rather than cyclical commodity dynamics.
- **Valuation demands perfection:** At $183.22, the market is pricing ~$521B in FY2030 revenue and sustained 60%+ EBIT margins. The Gordon Growth DCF implies $109.26 (−40% downside), while exit multiple and P/E comps bracket the range $91–$225. The risk/reward is asymmetric — limited upside if growth decelerates, severe downside if it does.

**Recommendation: HOLD** | **Target Price: $110** | **Downside: −40.0%**

## Top 3 Risks

1. **Demand concentration and cycle risk.** Hyperscaler capex accounts for the vast majority of Data Center revenue. Any pullback in cloud AI infrastructure spending (macro slowdown, ROI concerns on AI, regulatory restrictions on AI capex) would cause severe revenue deceleration. FY2023 proved NVIDIA is not immune to inventory cycles.

2. **Competitive displacement.** AMD MI300X, Google TPUs, Amazon Trainium, and Microsoft's custom silicon all target NVIDIA's inference workloads. If CUDA's moat proves weaker than assumed, or if open-source model architectures reduce compute requirements, the Data Center TAM supporting current multiples shrinks.

3. **Geopolitical / export restriction risk.** US export controls on advanced AI chips to China have already compressed addressable market. Further restrictions, or retaliatory measures, could reduce FY2026–FY2027 revenue by $10–15B vs. base case.

## Catalysts

- **Blackwell architecture ramp** (FY2026): GB200 NVL72 systems entering mass production; demand backlog reportedly exceeds 12-month supply
- **NVIDIA Inference Microservices (NIM):** software monetization layer that could structurally increase revenue per GPU deployed
- **Automotive (DRIVE Thor):** $11B+ in automotive design wins announced; revenue contribution grows from $1.7B (FY2025) to ~$2.5B (FY2026F)
- **Sovereign AI spending:** Government-funded AI infrastructure programs in EU, Middle East, Japan creating demand outside hyperscaler cycle
